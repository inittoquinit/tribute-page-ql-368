Edwin Catmull

Co-Founder of Pixar

Ed Catmull, photographed in 2019 for a BBC interview

1945: Born in Parkersburg, West Virginia, where he grew up watching Disney movies and dreamed of becoming an animator

1969: Graduates with a B.S. in physics and computer science from the University of Utah, where he became interested in digital imaging

1972: Creates a one-minute film called A Computer Animated Hand, where he created a 3D scan of his hand that was digitally animated

1976: His animated hand scan is incorporated into the film Futureworld, becoming the first film to use 3D computer graphics

1974: Earns a doctorate in computer science, with a thesis on a new subdivision algorithm for curved surfaces

1979: Becomes Vice President of Lucasfilm’s computer graphics division, where he worked on Star Wars’ CG effects

1986: Steve Jobs buys Lucasfilm’s digital division and together, they founded Pixar

1993: Receives a Scientific and Technical Award from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences for the creation of the PhotoRealistic RenderMan software

1995: Works as the Executive Producer on Toy Story, starting a long career working on Pixar movies

2007: A year after Disney acquires Pixar, he becomes the President of Disneytoon, and would later become the president of Walt Disney Animation Studios, encompassing Disneytoon, Disney Animation, and Pixar

2019: Retires from Disney Pixar, and is given the Turing Award for his contribution to computer-generated imagery

2022: Hired as the principal creative adviser for Thatgamecompany
